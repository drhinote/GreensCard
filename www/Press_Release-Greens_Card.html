Greens Card Launches Point-of-Sale Program for Cannabis Dispensaries
Transactions Facilitate Digital Payments for Cash-Intensive Industry
Las Vegas, Nev. Greens Card, a new alternative to cash for cannabis retailers and their
customers, employs a nontraditional network to facilitate safe, convenient and legal
transactions through a smartphone app.
Using the app is similar to using a credit or debit card, but the system is backed by a crypto-
currency network rather than companies like Visa or Master Card. And unlike a gift card,
Greens Card can be used at any location that accepts the program.
We want to establish an alternative to the traditional currency model of the cannabis
industry, said Greens Card founder Jeremy Higgins. We want to reduce the industrys
reliance on cash at the point of sale for convenience and security reasons while providing
customers the freedom to choose which dispensary to patronize.
The app is available to download for Windows, Android and Apple devices. After installing
it, customers are prompted to load funds from their bank account, which takes seconds to
do and becomes value stored in their account.
At the point of sale, value in a users account is converted in the amount of the transaction
to crypto-currency, which is used to purchase items from the dispensary. Dispensaries use
a QR code reader to interface with the customers smartphone and later exchange the
amount of crypto-currency earned in the transaction to dollars.
The process is easy and intuitive, Higgins said. Loading funds and completing retail
transactions happens instantly, and accounts can be funded while customers wait in line to
check out.
Because of the way the transactions are completed, Greens Card is not bound by
regulations impacting the retail cannabis or medical marijuana industries. The program is
legal under federal and Nevada laws, and it also adheres to regulations established by the
Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act.
We are not processing recreational cannabis transactions, and we are not sharing any
information other than an account and its value, Higgins said. After customers load funds
from their bank accounts into the app, we merely tender an amount to an account.
Greens Card is the first of its kind in the field. Because it is illegal for federally insured
banks to facilitate cannabis-related purchases, dispensaries have relied on cash
transactions.
Cash-intensive dispensaries have a higher risk of falling victim to robbery or theft, and
their employees face greater safety risks when storing or transporting large amounts of
cash, Higgins said. Dispensaries also cannot deposit cash from cannabis transactions into
traditional bank accounts, so they are limited in their means to transform cash into digital
currency.
The company uses two separate databases to protect users information. One database
securely stores the value of funds in an account, and the other securely stores the bank
account information in the users wallet.
The segregated databases provide an additional layer of security and a users account
cannot be connected to their banking information and/or purchases, Higgins said.
For dispensaries, pricing is transaction-based with a negotiated percentage based on the
payment terms. Daily payments from Greens Card to a dispensary, for example, carry a
higher fee than monthly payments.
There are no other fees or hidden costs, with the exception of purchasing a device that can
read a QR code if one does not already exist within the dispensary, Higgins said.
Greens Card is an open-loop, stored-value program that makes point-of-sale transactions
more convenient for customers and retailers alike via a smartphone app. It was developed
for the cannabis industry, but it can be used in other retail environments. Available to
download for Windows, Android and Apple devices, the app uses a crypto-currency
network to facilitate safe, secure and legal cannabis-related retail transactions.
